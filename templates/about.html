About

What this is, how it works, what each layer checks.

Most job search tools optimise for more applications. This one optimises for fewer, better ones. Every listing is evaluated across six layers before the question "is it worth applying?" is even asked — surfacing what the listing language reveals about the company and the role.

Six analysis layers
Triage
Role fit against your trajectory, initial signals, whether the employer is hidden behind a recruiter agency.
Product
What the product actually does, marketing claims vs. reality, AI-washing, verifiability of the mission.
Business
Revenue model, funding structure, investors, PE/VC involvement and what that typically signals.
Reputation
Uses the model's knowledge beyond the listing: Glassdoor/Indeed/Blind ratings and trends, dominant review themes, C-level history, layoff patterns, media, regulatory issues.
Values
Mission coherence, ethical traps, gap between stated values and observable behaviour.
Skills fit
Your profile against the role requirements: strengths to lead with, gaps, what to address in the application.
Reality check

Before the layer analysis the tool synthesises what the listing language actually signals about the role. Corpo-speak is decoded into plain English — phrases like "fast-paced environment" or "wear many hats" are translated to what they typically mean in practice. Purely informational; does not affect the verdict.

Verdicts
Worth considering No serious blockers. Layers are green or carry minor warnings only.
Needs review At least one warning layer or a yellow-list hit. Re-read before applying.
Rejected A flag, a zero-list match, or an AI-level concern. Confirm to archive.
