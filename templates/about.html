About

What this is, how it works, what each layer checks.

Most job search tools optimise for more applications. This one optimises for fewer, better ones. Every listing is evaluated across six layers before the question "is it worth applying?" is even asked — surfacing what the listing language reveals about the company and the role.

Job Screener never fetches content from job boards on your behalf. There is no scraping, no automated access to LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor, or any other platform. You paste the listing text yourself — you've already read it in your own browser, authenticated as yourself, on your own terms.

This is a deliberate design choice. Automated scraping of job boards sits in a legal and ethical grey zone even for personal use, and the grey zone shrinks considerably once money is involved. The paste-first approach is slower by one copy-paste, and completely unambiguous in every other dimension.

The URL field is optional. If provided, it is stored as a reference link in your personal history — so you can get back to the original posting — and nothing more. The tool never makes a network request to that address.

CV files uploaded in Settings are parsed in memory and never written to disk — see cv_parser.py on the public repo.

Six analysis layers
Triage
Role fit against your trajectory, initial signals, whether the employer is hidden behind a recruiter agency.
Product
What the product actually does, marketing claims vs. reality, AI-washing, verifiability of the mission.
Business
Revenue model, funding structure, investors, PE/VC involvement and what that typically signals.
Reputation
Uses the model's knowledge beyond the listing: Glassdoor/Indeed/Blind ratings and trends, dominant review themes, C-level history, layoff patterns, media, regulatory issues.
Values
Mission coherence, ethical traps, gap between stated values and observable behaviour.
Skills fit
Your profile against the role requirements: strengths to lead with, gaps, what to address in the application.

Before the layer analysis the tool decodes what the listing language actually signals about the role. Corpo-speak is translated to plain English — phrases like "fast-paced environment" or "wear many hats" are mapped to what they typically mean in practice. Purely informational; does not affect the verdict.

Verdicts & states
Worth considering No serious blockers. Layers are green or carry minor warnings only.
Needs review At least one warning layer or a yellow-list hit. Re-read before applying.
AI rejected AI assessed a flag or zero-list match. Unconfirmed — requires your manual confirmation to archive.
User rejected Rejection confirmed by you. Archived and struck through in history.
Applied You marked the application as submitted. Tracked in the statistics funnel.
Interview Interview scheduled. Marks the application as active in the funnel.
Offer received Offer stage reached.
Rejected by company Company declined after you applied. Closes the loop in the funnel.
